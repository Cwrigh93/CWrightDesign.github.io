Maxfit

~HCI, UX, Wireframing~

Introduction

Going to the gym is one of my favorite pastimes. It's said that we should treat our bodies as if they were a temple and I believe that this is something that should be followed. I've recognized with alot of people that ask me to guide or train them, the reasons in which they haven't embarked on a fitness journey. Some people are lazy while others are scared and confused. Schools don't give proper lessons on nutrition and can mislead students into thinking certain things are true while they aren't. The MaxFit app would help users take the step into the right direction when it comes to their health.

Reasons people don't go to the gym

Mockups

MaxFit incorporates the most crucial aspects of fitness. These include cardio, diet, and physical movement whether it be weight training, resistance, calisthenics, or plyometrics. As we learned earlier one of the biggest reasons people don't monitor their health is because they lack knowledge of these three areas of health and fitness. The other big reason is that people are scared to go to the gym because they have this preconceived notion that the normal gym-goers will look down upon them or they will embarrass themselves by looking confused. MaxFit combats all of these problems by giving the users programs and templates that are customizable through an easy to use interface. One of the best things about being active is that you don't necessarily need to be a member of a gym. The application will give users access to home-based training options as well as nutrition options.

The thing that separates this app from the rest is the versatility. There are apps such as BodySpace and MyFitnessPal that only do one thing and it's usually dedicated to that one task. Someone looking to start a health journey would have to download and use four or five different applications which can become annoying and tiresome. MaxFit is a one-stop for all one's fitness needs.
