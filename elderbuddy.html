The Elder Buddy

~HCI, UX, UX Research, UI Design, Rapid Prototyping~

Introduction

The Elder Buddy Served as my introduction into the world of User Experience and Interface Design. This project is what drove me to take the HCI route instead of information technology. The Elder Buddy is the result of the design process and criteria set by our professor that was to be met. We wanted to design a new app that would aid elderly individuals in everyday tasks that are crucial to their wellbeing. This was an interesting journey and a true challenge.

My Role

During this project which ran the entirety of the course, I held the role of a group leader. It was my job to push deadlines and oftentimes I found myself clarifying objectives for my fellow team members. It was quite an interesting experience since not only was I tasked with clarifying things but I was also simultaneously learning just as my teammates were.


First, there was the task of finding out what kind of application we should make. The professor of the course had guidelines that were to be followed. The main thing was to create an application that would be centered around helping old or elderly people. With this in mind, the question we had to ask ourselves was, “What is it that elderly people could use to help make their lives easier?”. As a group, we put our thoughts and ideas together to come up with something that we believed would help our target demographic.

Needfinding

As a group, this was our first needfinding phase ever. Due to the project being in class our needfinding started with simple interviews of other students centered around their grandparents. The individuals interviewed had grandparents over 65 years old. Health was the top theme we ran across while reviewing the data. Nine out of ten of the individuals we interviewed stated that their grandparents had to take some type of medication. The health problems varied and some of the individuals had multiple problems. About half of the interviewee’s grandparents used social media and a third of them used GPS to get home.

  • Multiple interviewers were on pain medications.
  • Health, dying and getting lost were accompanied by emotional responses.
  • Emotions and attitudes: likes, dislikes, perceptions- Accepting Old Age (fear)
  • Behavior: usage, habits, tools, etc. – smartphones, pain medication

Conclusion

As stated above the goal of our app is to make life easier for the elderly as the name suggests “Elder Buddy”. We mainly focused on three things: help them keep track of their medicine schedule, help them find their car, and give them the choice to share their location with family and friends. We made sure that the app is easy to navigate, has optimized user flow, and a design-friendly interface (finger-friendly) covering the goals of the usability. We evaluated our app using the wizard of oz interview and experimental usability study, where we received users input about the app, what they liked about and what they thought we should change. Overall they found the functionality of the app easy to understand and follow. Most of the suggestions for improvements were centered around making the app more visually appealing.



Once again, our design concept was intended to be far from complex. It incorporates a smart device with interaction through human touch. Touch and sight for real-time feedback are the only things necessary for the interaction since our app doesn’t incorporate sound or video streaming. The biggest thing to take into consideration was the ages of our users and what kind of previous experience they have had with technology. As a result of our research and implementation based on the feedback received from target users, our application was closer to being a success.
