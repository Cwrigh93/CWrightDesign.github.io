Pictionary Digtial: Rapid Prototyping

~HCI, UX, UX Research, UI, Wireframing, Prototyping~
Sketching

Introduction

This project was centered around design patterns, tools and the implementation of each. My group had to decided on a board game and redesign it using the tools and design patterns that we were taught. By the end of the project, we were expected to have a semi-functional prototype in which a single round of the game could be simulated.

My Role

During this project I didn't assume the role of leader but I did guide us in the beginning because I had knowledge of User interfaces and User Experience. There were three of us in a group but one member didn't pull his weight at all, therefore I had to step up in order to complete his assigned task as well. While we learned new design patterns and practices it was up to us to implement them into our User Interface.

Implemented Features

  • Canvas and Pallete
  • Coin Flip and dice rolls
  • Card scecrecy
  • Team editing
  • Timer
  • Leaderboard
  • Rulebook and search
  • Undo and redo for mistake correction

Approach

My partner and I decided to go with Pictionary. Pictionary is a game where teams draw things that must be guessed by other people. This game doesn't involve technology but we intended to make it to where players could use Ipads and tablets to play without having to deal with physical pieces. Going into this project I had worked with Balsamiq and Axure while my partner had experience with Balsamiq.

The Difference between platforms

There was a lot to introduce and keep in mind when we were attempting to convert a hands on game such as pictionary into a digital form. It was also challenging to meet all of the required criteria set by the professor. Some of the design patterns we were supposed to implement didn't align with our vision for the game, nor were they necessary components.


Final Prototype

Conclusion

This experience was very fulfilling in terms of knowledge and experience. We used many difference types of prototyping tools and methods such as Axure, powerpoint and video prototyping which I had never been introduced to. I believe this course and project will impact my professional career more than any other course I have taken during my time as a undergraduate.



I would be lying if I said that we didn't run into any problems. The problem with the team member that didn't care nor try to carry their weight is something that I would expect to see once I actually join the work force. It's great (in a sense) that I have exposure to such an occurrence and know ways to deal with it. One thing I wish that could've been different is that we had a chance to do more user testing on our product as well as pick something entirely different then what was within the criteria.
